Shortest Job First (Non-Preemptive)

When the CPU is available, it is assigned to the process in the Ready Queue that has the smallest Burst Time. Because it is Non-Preemptive, once a process begins execution, it cannot be interrupted until it completes, even if a shorter job arrives.

SJF Exam Theory

Characteristics

  • Criteria: Burst Time (BT). Shorter jobs are executed first.
  • Mode: Non-Preemptive. A running process will not release the CPU until its burst is complete.
  • Tie-Breaker: If two processes have the same Burst Time, FCFS (Arrival Time) is used to break the tie.

The Convoy Effect Trap

SJF is designed to fix the Convoy Effect. However, because it is non-preemptive, if a massive job arrives at Time 0, and shorter jobs arrive at Time 1, the CPU is already locked by the massive job. The short jobs will still have to wait! (See Dataset 3).

Formulas

  • Turnaround Time (TAT): Completion Time - Arrival Time
  • Waiting Time (WT): Turnaround Time - Burst Time
