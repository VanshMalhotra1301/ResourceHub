Round Robin (RR) Scheduling

Designed specifically for timesharing systems. Each process is assigned a fixed time slot called a Time Quantum (TQ). If a process does not complete within its TQ, it is preempted and placed at the back of the Ready Queue. It guarantees fairness and prevents starvation.

Round Robin Theory

The Rule of TQ

The performance of Round Robin depends heavily on the size of the Time Quantum (TQ).

  • If TQ is very large: RR behaves exactly like FCFS.
  • If TQ is very small: The system spends too much time context switching, leading to high overhead and poor performance.

The Preemption Queue Rule

If a process finishes its time quantum at Time T, and a new process arrives exactly at Time T, the newly arriving process is added to the Ready Queue before the preempted process is pushed to the back.

Formulas

  • Turnaround Time (TAT): Completion Time - Arrival Time
  • Waiting Time (WT): Turnaround Time - Burst Time
